2. **Central Bank Purchases**: Central banks have been significant buyers of gold, with China emerging as the largest buyer. This trend is driven by gold's lack of default risk and its status as a neutral reserve asset, especially after sanctions on Russia highlighted vulnerabilities in dollar-centric reserves (per CNBC).

3. **Geopolitical Tensions**: The ongoing geopolitical tensions, including the Israel-Hamas ceasefire and US-China trade disputes, have increased gold's appeal as a safe-haven asset. These tensions have led to a flight to safety, boosting gold prices (per Benzinga and Bloomberg).

4. **US Dollar Dynamics**: The depreciation of the US dollar has been a critical factor in gold's price surge. The dollar's decline has made gold cheaper for holders of other currencies, thereby increasing demand (per ZeroHedge).

1. **Profit-Taking and Market Correction**: There is a risk of profit-taking as gold prices have surged rapidly. A pullback from the $4,000 level is expected, which could lead to a temporary market correction (per CNBC). The probability of this occurring is high in the short term as investors lock in gains.

2. **Increased Correlation with Other Asset Classes**: If gold's correlation with other asset classes increases, its appeal as a diversifier may diminish. This could lead to reduced demand from investors seeking uncorrelated assets (per CNBC).
