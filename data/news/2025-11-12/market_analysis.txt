# Key Price Drivers and Catalysts

- **Fed Policy**: The potential for a more dovish Federal Reserve stance is a significant driver. The CME FedWatch Tool indicates a 63% probability of a December rate cut, down from nearly 100% in October (per Barchart). A dovish Fed could further support gold prices.

- **USD Dynamics**: The U.S. dollar is in a downtrend, with a projected decline of 13.5% over the remainder of Donald Trump's term (per Barchart). This depreciation is supportive of higher gold prices.

- **Central Bank Purchases**: Central banks continue to add gold to their reserves, driven by geopolitical tensions and the need for diversification. This trend is expected to persist, supporting gold prices (per Barchart).

- **Physical Demand and ETF Flows**: Renewed inflows into gold ETFs signal increased demand for the metal, contributing to the bullish sentiment (per Barchart).

- **Technical Factors**: Gold's recent rebound from $4,125 per ounce and its positive correlation with equities suggest a favorable technical setup (per Benzinga and Bloomberg).

- **Regulatory Changes in Crypto**: The advancement of the crypto market structure bill could shift investor focus back to digital assets, potentially reducing demand for gold (per Benzinga). The bill is expected to progress by late 2025, with a vote in early 2026.

- **Economic Data Surprises**: Strong economic data could lead to a more hawkish Fed, negatively impacting gold prices. The probability of such surprises remains moderate, with mitigation possible through diversified investment strategies.
