# Key Price Drivers and Catalysts

- **Fed Policy**: Changes in U.S. government policies and Federal Reserve actions significantly impact gold prices. Lower interest rates and quantitative easing tend to boost gold as an inflation hedge (per YahooRSS).

- **USD Dynamics**: A weaker US dollar typically supports higher gold prices. The inverse relationship means that any depreciation in the dollar could lead to a rise in gold prices (per YahooRSS).

- **Inflation Data**: Gold acts as a hedge against inflation. As inflation rises, the purchasing power of currency diminishes, making gold more attractive (per YahooRSS).

- **Geopolitics**: Political tensions and uncertainties drive investors towards gold as a safe haven. Recent geopolitical events have contributed to gold's price increase over the past decade (per YahooRSS).

- **Central Bank Purchases**: While not explicitly mentioned, central bank purchases often support gold prices by increasing demand (general market knowledge).

- **Mining Supply**: Supply constraints can lead to higher prices, although specific data on mining output was not provided in the articles.

- **Physical Demand and ETF Flows**: The demand for physical gold and flows into gold ETFs like GLD and AAAU influence prices. AAAU's lower expense ratio makes it attractive for long-term investors, potentially increasing demand (per YahooRSS).

- **Technical Factors**: Gold's price movement is influenced by technical trading levels. Support at $4,000 and resistance at $4,341 are key levels to watch (per YahooRSS).
