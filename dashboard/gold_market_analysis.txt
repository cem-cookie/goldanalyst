# Key Price Drivers and Catalysts

- **Fed Policy and Interest Rates**: The prospect of interest rate cuts has diminished due to heightened inflation risks from geopolitical tensions, notably the US-Iran standoff. This has been a headwind for gold, which does not yield interest (per Bloomberg).

- **USD Dynamics**: The US dollar's recent strength, as indicated by a 0.1% rise in the Bloomberg Dollar Spot Index, has pressured gold prices downward. The dollar's movements are closely tied to geopolitical developments and trade tensions, such as potential tariff hikes on EU automobiles (per Bloomberg).

- **Geopolitical Tensions**: The ongoing US-Iran conflict has significantly impacted gold prices, contributing to a 14% decline since February. The closure of the Strait of Hormuz and the resulting energy price shock have affected market expectations for interest rate cuts (per Bloomberg).

- **Central Bank Purchases**: Central banks have been accumulating gold at the fastest pace in over a year, providing a medium-term bullish outlook. This trend is expected to continue supporting gold prices despite short-term volatility (per Bloomberg).

- **Physical Demand**: Continued retail buying in China has helped support gold prices, indicating strong physical demand in key markets (per Bloomberg).

- **USD Appreciation**: A stronger US dollar poses a risk to gold prices, as it makes gold more expensive for international buyers. This risk is closely tied to US economic policies and geopolitical developments.
