# Key Price Drivers and Catalysts

- **Fed Policy**: The Federal Reserve's monetary policy remains a key driver. Any indication of rate hikes could pressure gold prices, while dovish stances support them. The articles did not provide specific Fed meeting dates or outcomes.

- **USD Dynamics**: The US dollar's weakness, driven by federal debt concerns, supports gold prices. The devaluation of the dollar due to prolific debt accumulation is cited as a catalyst for gold's rise (per YahooRSS).

- **Inflation Data**: Elevated inflation rates, particularly post-Iran war, have kept inflation above 3%, reinforcing gold's role as an inflation hedge (per YahooRSS).

- **Geopolitics**: Geopolitical tensions, including the Iran war, have contributed to gold's appeal as a safe haven asset.

- **Central Bank Purchases**: Central banks remain net buyers of gold, providing a solid demand base. Although purchases have decreased from recent highs, they still support the market (per YahooRSS).

- **Mining Supply**: Companies like G Mining Ventures and West Red Lake Gold Mines are adjusting production targets, with G Mining Ventures maintaining a 2026 guidance of 160,000 to 190,000 ounces, despite Q1 output dips (per YahooRSS).

- **Physical Demand and ETF Flows**: The SPDR Gold MiniShares Trust ETF (GLDM) is highlighted for its lower expense ratio, attracting retail investors. Its performance, with a 22.1% average annual return over five years, underscores strong ETF demand (per YahooRSS).
